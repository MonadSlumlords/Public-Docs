# 📈 Corporate Strategy: Min-Maxing Misery

**"Base yield is for amateurs. Optimization is where the real capital is forged."**

Welcome to the big leagues, Junior. Slumlords is not a passive idle game; it is an active, hyper-competitive economic simulation. If you just randomly stake your properties and walk away to play golf, the protocol's fatigue and tax systems will slowly bleed your portfolio dry.

To dominate the ecosystem and extract maximum value from your tenants, you must master the following operational strategies.

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### 🤝 Synergy Exploitation (Zoning Collusion)

Never randomly group properties. The district a property is located in heavily dictates your bottom line.

* **The Exploit:** Staking specific district pairs (e.g., _Foreclosure Fields_ + _Landlord Lagoon_) activates hidden, hardcoded protocol multipliers.
* **The Action:** Always check the `SYNERGY_NETWORK` panel on your Dashboard. Buy, trade, or mint until you complete these exact pairings to secure massive passive buffs like +15% Yield or -10% Fatigue accumulation. Build monopolies, not mixed neighborhoods.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### 🧮 The Maintenance Math (Sociopathic Accounting)

Property fatigue reduces your gross yield, but resetting it costs exactly 15% of your portfolio's modified base yield. You must strip all emotion and "tenant empathy" from your decision-making.

* **The Trap:** Constantly repairing your properties the second they hit 5% fatigue will literally bankrupt you via reset fees.
* **The Action:** Rely strictly on the Finance module's `MAINTENANCE_ROI_ANALYZER`. Let your properties degrade until the daily `$RENT` you are losing to the fatigue penalty significantly outweighs the flat cost to reset it. If the math says let the pipes leak for another week, you let them leak.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### 🏢 Capital Density (Vertical Slumming)

Because you are strictly hard-capped at a maximum of 20 properties (and that's only _if_ you have fully maxed out your Staking Expansion skills), you cannot scale infinitely by just minting more real estate. Horizontal sprawl has a limit.

* **The Exploit:** You must scale _vertically_. Acquire Upgrades from the `UPGRADE_CATALOG` and physically attach them to your properties.
* **The Action:** Target Tier 3 (T3) properties, which feature 4 available upgrade slots. Stack your highest-yielding upgrades onto these premium properties to create hyper-dense capital extractors. Just remember: upgrades are subject to the same structural fatigue decay as the base property, so monitor their maintenance closely or those expensive upgrades will rot into nothing.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### 💼 Bribing the Protocol (Skill Points)

Do not neglect the operator. You can permanently alter your account's macroeconomic rules by purchasing Skill Points (essentially lobbying the Monad city council).

* **The Trap:** Skill Points use a vicious quadratic bonding curve. The first point costs a measly 50 `$RENT`; the 10th point will cost you a small fortune. You cannot buy your way to the top instantly.
* **The Action:** Prioritize your early, cheap Skill Points wisely based on your current bottlenecks:
  * If you are hitting your 10-property cap, dump points immediately into **Staking Expansion** to open new slots.
  * If you have highly upgraded properties generating massive income, max out **Tax Reduction** to stop the protocol from siphoning 25% of your gross yield back into the burn wallet.
{% endstep %}
