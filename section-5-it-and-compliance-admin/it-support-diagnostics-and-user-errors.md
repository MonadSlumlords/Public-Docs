# 🚨 IT Support: Diagnostics & User Errors

**"The system isn't broken. You just didn't read the documentation."**

If your terminal is throwing errors or your portfolio is drastically underperforming, consult this diagnostic log before submitting a network ticket. (Spoiler: The IT department will just tell you to read this anyway, and then they'll charge you a consulting fee).

***

## 📉 Yield & Extraction Errors (Why You Are Poor)

<details>

<summary><strong>Q: Why is my Net Yield per day showing exactly 0?</strong></summary>

**A:** You have completely neglected your property. If your property's Fatigue reaches the hard cap of 99%, the smart contract mathematically zeroes out your gross yield. Run a `RESET FATIGUE` operation immediately in the Maintenance Panel to fix the collapsing roof. Alternatively, if your Protocol Tax has reached 100% due to terrible district combinations, the protocol is taking everything and you will extract nothing.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Q: I attached a massive, expensive upgrade, but my yield barely went up?</strong></summary>

**A:** Upgrades are subject to the base property's structural fatigue decay. If you attach a +400 `$RENT` upgrade to a property sitting at 80% fatigue, the upgrade's output is instantly throttled by 80%. Fix the actual building before installing the luxury smart-home system.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Q: I reset my Skill Points and all my properties disappeared! Did I get hacked?</strong></summary>

**A:** No, you just didn't read the corporate restructuring manual. They didn't disappear; they were forcefully returned to your local "WALLET" tab. When you execute a `resetSkillPoints` transaction, the contract triggers a violent **Auto-Eject** protocol to prevent you from illegally holding more properties than your new capacity limits allow. It automatically claims your pending yield, rips your upgrades off the walls, and unstakes everything. You must manually re-stake your portfolio and apologize to your tenants.

</details>

## ⛓️ Transaction & Blockchain Errors (The Monad Dark Forest)

<details>

<summary><strong>Q: Why did my MINT transaction fail? I paid the gas!</strong></summary>

**A:** There are three primary reasons a mint reverts on-chain:

Insufficient $RENTYou have the MON, but you didn't check the current $RENT token requirement, which aggressively increases every 5,000 units.Missing AllowanceYou must click "AUTHORIZE RENT" before minting to give the contract permission to physically burn your tokens. We can't incinerate your money if you don't hand us the lighter. Supply Cap The total global supply of 20,000 properties has been reached, or you are greedily trying to mint more than the per-transaction limit (20).

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Q: My DEX Swap failed through the OS router.</strong></summary>

**A:** This is almost always a slippage issue. The `LIQUIDITY_ROUTER` applies a tight 0.5% slippage tolerance to protect you from predatory MEV sandwich bots roaming the Monad network. If the market is highly volatile, your transaction may revert to save your capital. Wait a few seconds for the Oracle to re-sync, or manually adjust your trade size.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Q: Why can't I attach an upgrade to my property? The button is greyed out.</strong></summary>

**A:** Check the property's Tier. A Tier 1 (T1) shoebox property only has 2 upgrade slots. If those slots are full, the transaction will revert. You cannot fit a helipad on a garden shed. You must `DETACH` an old upgrade before installing a new one.

</details>
