## 📊 TOKENOMICS

Listen closely, Junior. Slumlords operates on a strictly enforced, dual-asset economic model. The ecosystem is mathematically designed to reward active extraction and severely penalize passive holding. Understanding where the capital flows is the only way to ensure your portfolio survives the protocol's continuous deflationary sinks.

Here is how the BlackCoq money machine operates.

***

### 🏦 The Native Asset: `MON` (Baseline Capital)

`MON` is the baseline currency of the Monad network and acts as the foundational capital required to interact with the OS.

* **The Toll Booth (Gas Fees):** All ledger interactions (Minting, Staking, Upgrading, Claiming) require microscopic amounts of native `MON` to process.
* **The Bribe (Whitelist Authorization):** Purchasing a priority Whitelist Spot requires a flat upfront fee. 100% of this fee is routed directly to seed the initial decentralized exchange liquidity pool.
* **The Extraction (Acquisition Base Fees):** Minting new properties and purchasing physical upgrades require a base `MON` payment. A 5% brokerage fee is extracted to fund BlackCoq protocol operations, and the remaining 95% is autonomously routed to the liquidity router to execute massive market-buys of `$RENT`.

***

### 🩸 The Utility Asset: `$RENT` (Extraction Token)

`$RENT` is an ERC-20 token continuously emitted by your staked properties. It is the lifeblood of the Slumlords simulation. However, the smart contracts are heavily weaponized to pull `$RENT` out of circulation just as fast as it is minted.

**Where the protocol forces you to spend it:**

1. **Public Property Minting:** Expanding your baseline real estate portfolio requires burning `$RENT` alongside your `MON` fee.
2. **Property Upgrades:** Acquiring high-yield renovations (like slapping a fresh coat of paint over black mold) requires massive, escalating `$RENT` payments.
3. **Skill Acquisition (Lobbying):** Permanently upgrading your operator parameters costs `$RENT`, governed by a brutal quadratic pricing curve.
4. **Maintenance (Fatigue Resets):** Repairing structural decay costs exactly 15% of your portfolio's modified base yield.
5. **Consumables (Steroids):** Purchasing temporary 24-hour modifiers (Slow Fatigue, Boost Yield) requires dynamic `$RENT` payments based on your gross yield.
6. **Administrative Respecs:** Resetting your operator Skill Points triggers a massive 200% daily yield administrative penalty.

**🔥 The Deflationary Law:**\
`$RENT` spent inside the Slumlords OS does not go into a developer treasury. It does not go into a "community rewards pool". **100% of `$RENT` utilized for Minting, Upgrades, Skills, Maintenance, and Taxes is permanently destroyed via the contract's `burn` function\`.**
